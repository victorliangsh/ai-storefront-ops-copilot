# Product Selection Framework

This framework helps ecommerce operators evaluate product ideas before building a Shopify or direct-to-consumer storefront.

The goal is not to find a perfect product. The goal is to quickly reject weak ideas and focus on products that are easier to explain, demonstrate, source, and test.

## Core Selection Criteria

A strong product candidate should meet most of the following criteria.

## 1. Clear Customer Problem

The product should solve a problem that customers can understand quickly.

Good signs:

- The problem is visible
- The problem is common
- The customer already knows they have the problem
- The product benefit can be explained in one sentence

Example:

Pet hair shedding is visible on clothes, sofas, rugs, and bedding. A grooming tool can be explained quickly because the problem is easy to recognize.

## 2. Strong Visual Demonstration

The product should be easy to show in a short video or product image.

Good signs:

- Before-and-after result
- Visible use case
- Simple demonstration
- Easy to understand without long explanation

A product that needs too much education may be harder to test quickly with social traffic.

## 3. Simple Sourcing

The product should be easy to source, inspect, and ship.

Good signs:

- Multiple suppliers exist
- Product is not fragile
- Product is not too heavy
- Product does not require complex installation
- Product has clear photos or demo assets

## 4. Healthy Margin Potential

The product should have enough pricing room after cost, shipping, payment fees, and advertising tests.

Operators should estimate:

- Product cost
- Shipping cost
- Payment fees
- Packaging cost
- Expected selling price
- Testing budget

A useful early filter is whether the product can support a practical markup after all major costs.

## 5. Low Compliance and Return Risk

Avoid products that create unnecessary risk for a small seller.

Higher-risk categories may include:

- Medical claims
- Safety-sensitive electronics
- Children’s safety products
- Cosmetics with strong claims
- Products requiring regulated certifications
- Products with high return probability

## 6. One-Product Store Fit

Some products are easier to sell through a focused one-product storefront.

Good signs:

- The product solves one clear problem
- The page can focus on one main offer
- The product can support a simple FAQ
- The benefit can be shown near the purchase button
- The store does not need a large catalog to make sense

## Product Scorecard

Operators can score each product from 1 to 5.

| Criteria | Score |
| --- | --- |
| Clear customer problem | 1-5 |
| Strong visual demonstration | 1-5 |
| Simple sourcing | 1-5 |
| Healthy margin potential | 1-5 |
| Low compliance risk | 1-5 |
| Low return risk | 1-5 |
| One-product store fit | 1-5 |
| Short-form content potential | 1-5 |

## Decision Guide

- 32+ points: Strong candidate for further research
- 25-31 points: Possible candidate, needs review
- Below 25 points: Usually not worth building first

## Notes

This framework is designed for early-stage screening. Operators should still validate supplier quality, shipping times, legal requirements, payment readiness, and customer demand before launching.
