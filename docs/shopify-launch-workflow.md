# Shopify Launch Workflow

This workflow describes a practical process for launching a focused Shopify or direct-to-consumer product page.

The goal is to help small operators move from product idea to launch-ready storefront without losing track of the important steps.

## Step 1: Product Screening

Start by reviewing whether the product is suitable for a focused storefront.

Check:

- Clear customer problem
- Strong visual demonstration
- Simple sourcing
- Healthy margin potential
- Low compliance risk
- Low return risk
- Short-form content potential

## Step 2: Supplier Review

Before building the product page, review the supplier information.

Check:

- Product cost
- Shipping options
- Minimum order quantity
- Available product photos
- Available product videos
- Product variants
- Packaging information
- Customer reviews or supplier history

## Step 3: Offer Planning

Define the offer before writing the product page.

Prepare:

- Product name
- Target customer
- Main customer problem
- Main product benefit
- Price range
- Shipping message
- Guarantee or return policy
- Bundle or discount plan

## Step 4: Product Page Draft

Create the first product page draft.

Include:

- Product title
- Short description
- Key benefits
- Product images or demo assets
- FAQ
- Trust section
- Shipping information
- Return or guarantee message

## Step 5: Storefront Readiness Review

Before sending traffic, check the storefront.

Review:

- Mobile layout
- Add-to-cart button
- Variant selector
- Product image quality
- Price visibility
- Checkout flow
- Payment method readiness
- Shipping information
- Contact or support path

## Step 6: Content Preparation

Prepare content angles for organic or paid traffic.

Examples:

- Problem demonstration
- Before-and-after usage
- Product routine
- Customer pain point
- Simple product demo
- Comparison against old solution

## Step 7: Launch Test

Start with a small test and track simple signals.

Track:

- Page visits
- Add-to-cart activity
- Checkout activity
- Sales
- Customer questions
- Ad creative performance
- Product-page issues

## Step 8: Daily Review

Use a daily operations review to decide what to fix next.

Review:

- What worked today?
- What confused customers?
- Did the page load and work correctly?
- Did visitors add to cart?
- Did checkout work?
- What content angle should be tested next?

## Step 9: Improve or Stop

Do not keep improving a weak product forever.

Possible decisions:

- Improve product page
- Test new content angle
- Adjust offer
- Replace product images
- Change supplier
- Stop the product and test a new one

## Notes

This workflow is designed for fast learning. It helps operators launch in a structured way without pretending that every product will work.
